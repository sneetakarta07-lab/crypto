Buying Bitcoin in Japan is simple if you follow a safe process. Most beginners lose money not because Bitcoin is “bad,” but because they rush: they download fake apps, skip security settings, or use the wrong order type. This guide is written to help you buy BTC safely, understand fees, and avoid common mistakes in 2026.

Step 1: Choose a reputable exchange

Start by choosing a trusted exchange. In Japan, users care about security, transparency, and a smooth deposit/withdraw experience. If you haven’t decided yet, read our main comparison: Best crypto exchange in Japan (2026).

  • Security: 2FA, withdrawal protection, device management.
  • Clear fees: spot fees, withdrawal fees, and spreads.
  • Liquidity: better pricing and less slippage for BTC/ETH.

Step 2: Create your account + lock security

Use a unique password and enable 2FA immediately. This single step prevents most account takeovers. Also enable email/login alerts and review “connected devices” settings.

Step 3: Complete KYC verification

On serious platforms, identity verification (KYC) is normal. It helps reduce fraud and improves withdrawal limits. Keep your details consistent and avoid uploading unclear photos. After KYC, you’ll be able to deposit and trade smoothly.

Step 4: Deposit funds (start small)

Beginners should deposit a small amount first. Your goal is to learn the flow: deposit → buy BTC → sell BTC → withdraw. Once you understand the steps, you can increase your size.

Tip: Always check deposit/withdraw methods and costs. Fees are explained in our main guide: fees section.

Step 5: Buy Bitcoin (market vs limit)

You’ll usually trade a BTC pair (example: BTC/USDT). Two common order types:

  • Market order: instant buy at current price. Simple, but can be slightly worse in fast markets.
  • Limit order: you set the price. Better control; recommended once you understand basics.

Safety checklist (Japan beginners)

  • Bookmark official URLs. Avoid random links in DMs.
  • Enable 2FA + anti-phishing code if available.
  • Do a small test withdrawal before moving large funds.
  • Never share OTP codes or recovery phrases.

FAQs

Is buying Bitcoin legal in Japan?

Yes, buying and selling Bitcoin is legal in Japan. Reputable platforms commonly require KYC.

How much should a beginner start with?

Start small. The first goal is learning the process safely, not “getting rich fast.”

Should I keep BTC on an exchange?

For long-term storage, many users prefer a personal wallet. For small learning amounts, exchange storage can be acceptable if security is strong.
